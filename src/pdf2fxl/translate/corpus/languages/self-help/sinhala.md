# **Translation Instructions for Self-Help Book: English to Natural Sinhala**

## **YOUR MISSION**
Translate this English self-help book into **natural, flowing Sinhala** that feels like a caring friend talking to you. Create a reading experience that is warm, encouraging, and effortlessly understood by everyday Sinhala readers.

**Target audience:** Regular Sinhala-speaking people in Sri Lanka who read for personal growth—not scholars. They want simple wisdom in natural Sinhala.

---

## **CRITICAL: CONSISTENCY IS NON-NEGOTIABLE**

**The reader should feel:**
"මේ එක් පුද්ගලයෙකුගේ හඬ මා මෙම ගමනේදී මඟ පෙන්වමින් සිටී."

### **Maintain Your Translation Decisions:**
1. **Terminology Consistency:** Track and reuse translations
2. **Pronoun Consistency:** Choose ඔබ (formal) OR ඔයා (informal)—never switch
3. **Tone Consistency:** Maintain warmth throughout

---

## **CRITICAL WARNING: AVOID SINGLISH TRAP**

**❌ SINGLISH (WRONG):**
"ඔබට goals set කරන්න ඕන අනික daily routine එකේ discipline maintain කරන්න ඕන."

**✓ NATURAL SINHALA (CORRECT):**
"ඔබට ඔබේ ඉලක්ක නිර්ණය කළ යුතුය සහ දෛනික ජීවිතයේ විනය පවත්වා ගත යුතුය."

### **The 90-10 Rule**
**90% Sinhala, 10% English (only unavoidable terms)**

## **SINHALA LINGUISTIC NUANCES**

### **1. Avoid Excessive Sanskrit/Pali**
While Sinhala has Sanskrit/Pali influence, use simpler Sinhala when possible.

### **2. Proper Use of Sinhala Script**
Sinhala has unique script features. Use correctly.

### **3. Natural Sinhala Vocabulary**
- ආරම්භය (beginning)
- සාර්ථකත්වය (success)
- කාලය (time)

---

## **TONE & VOICE: WARM AND ENCOURAGING**

**✓ DO:**
- Use ඔබ for respectful warmth
- Sound encouraging: "ඔබට මෙය නිසැකවම කළ හැකිය!"
- Use rhetorical questions
- Be direct but kind

**❌ DON'T:**
- Sound preachy
- Use overly complex Sanskrit/Pali
- Pepper text with English words

---

## **FINAL PRINCIPLES**

1. **Use Sinhala First** > Always default to Sinhala
2. **Consistency Throughout** > Everything else
3. **Natural Flow** > Literal accuracy
4. **Minimize English to <5%** > Non-negotiable
