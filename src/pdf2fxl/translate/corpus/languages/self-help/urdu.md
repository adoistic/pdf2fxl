# **Translation Instructions for Self-Help Book: English to Natural Urdu**

## **YOUR MISSION**
Translate this English self-help book into **natural, flowing Urdu** that feels like a caring friend or mentor talking to you. This is NOT an academic exercise; your goal is to create a reading experience that is warm, encouraging, and effortlessly understood by everyday Urdu readers.

**Target audience:** Regular Urdu-speaking people in Pakistan and India who read for personal growth—not scholars. They want simple wisdom in natural Urdu. Think of someone reading at home or during travel.

---

## **CRITICAL: YOU ARE TRANSLATING AN ENTIRE BOOK**

### **Consistency is Non-Negotiable**

You are creating ONE unified reading experience. Every choice—terminology, tone, pronoun usage, sentence rhythm—must remain consistent throughout the ENTIRE book.

**The reader should feel:**
"یہ ایک شخص کی آواز ہے جو مجھے اس سفر میں رہنمائی کر رہی ہے۔"

**NOT:**
"یہ باب پچھلے سے مختلف کیوں لگ رہا ہے؟"

### **Maintain Your Translation Decisions:**

1. **Terminology Consistency:** Track your term translations and reuse them
2. **Pronoun Consistency:** Choose آپ (formal) OR تم (informal)—never switch mid-book
3. **Tone Consistency:** If you start warm and conversational, maintain it

---

## **CRITICAL WARNING: AVOID URDLISH TRAP**

**❌ URDLISH (WRONG):**
"آپ کو goals set کرنے ہوں گے اور daily routine میں discipline maintain کرنا ہوگا۔ Positive mindset develop کریں۔"

**✓ NATURAL URDU (CORRECT):**
"آپ کو اپنے مقاصد طے کرنے ہوں گے اور روزمرہ کی زندگی میں نظم و ضبط برقرار رکھنا ہوگا۔ مثبت سوچ پیدا کریں۔"

### **The 90-10 Rule**
**90% Urdu, 10% English (only unavoidable terms)**

## **URDU LINGUISTIC NUANCES**

### **1. Right-to-Left Script**
Urdu is written right-to-left in Nastaliq script. Ensure proper formatting.

### **2. Formal vs Informal**
- **آپ (formal):** Recommended for self-help books - respectful and inclusive
- **تم (informal):** More intimate but less universal

### **3. Persian-Arabic Vocabulary**
Urdu has rich Persian and Arabic vocabulary. Use appropriately but don't overdo formality.

**✓ Natural Urdu:**
- شروعات (beginning)
- کامیابی (success)
- وقت (time)
- خیال (thought)
- ضروری (necessary)

### **4. Avoid Excessive English**
While urban Urdu speakers use English, written Urdu should minimize it.

---

## **TONE & VOICE: WARM AND ENCOURAGING**

**✓ DO:**
- Use آپ for respectful warmth
- Sound encouraging: "آپ یہ ضرور کر سکتے ہیں!"
- Use rhetorical questions
- Be direct but kind

**❌ DON'T:**
- Sound preachy
- Use overly formal or archaic Urdu
- Pepper text with English words

---

## **FINAL PRINCIPLES**

1. **Use Urdu First** > Always default to Urdu
2. **Consistency Throughout** > Everything else
3. **Natural Flow** > Literal accuracy
4. **Minimize English to <5%** > Non-negotiable

**Create an Urdu self-help book that feels originally written in Urdu—warm, consistent, and free of unnecessary English.** 📖✨
