# **Translation Instructions for Self-Help Book: English to Natural Bengali**

## **YOUR MISSION**
Translate this English self-help book into **natural, flowing Bengali** that feels like a caring friend or elder talking to you. This is NOT an academic exercise; your goal is to create a reading experience that is warm, encouraging, and effortlessly understood by everyday Bengali readers.

**Target audience:** Regular Bengali-speaking people in West Bengal and Bangladesh who read for personal growth—not scholars. They want simple wisdom in natural Bengali (Cholito Bhasha). Think of someone reading at home or during their commute.

---

## **CRITICAL: YOU ARE TRANSLATING AN ENTIRE BOOK**

### **Consistency is Non-Negotiable**

You are creating ONE unified reading experience. Every choice—terminology, tone, pronoun usage, sentence rhythm—must remain consistent throughout the ENTIRE book.

**The reader should feel:**
"এটা একটা মানুষের কথা যে আমাকে এই যাত্রায় পথ দেখাচ্ছে।"

**NOT:**
"এই অধ্যায়টা আগেরটার থেকে আলাদা কেন লাগছে?"

### **Maintain Your Translation Decisions:**

1. **Terminology Consistency:**
   - If you translate "mindset" as "মানসিকতা" in Chapter 1, use it throughout
   - If you choose "সাফল্য" for success, don't switch to "কৃতিত্ব" randomly
   - Track your term translations and reuse them

2. **Pronoun Consistency:**
   - Choose আপনি (formal) OR তুমি (informal) OR তুই (very informal)—never switch mid-book
   - আপনি is recommended for wider appeal

3. **Tone Consistency:**
   - If you start warm and conversational, maintain it

---

## **CRITICAL WARNING: AVOID BANGLISH TRAP**

### **This is THE MOST COMMON MISTAKE - READ CAREFULLY**

**The Problem:** Translators keep too many English words, creating awkward Banglish instead of natural Bengali.

**❌ BANGLISH (WRONG):**
"আপনাকে goals set করতে হবে আর daily routine-এ discipline maintain করতে হবে। Positive mindset develop করুন আর negative thoughts avoid করুন। Success-এর জন্য hard work জরুরি।"

**✓ NATURAL BENGALI (CORRECT):**
"আপনাকে লক্ষ্য ঠিক করতে হবে আর রোজকার জীবনে শৃঙ্খলা বজায় রাখতে হবে। ইতিবাচক মানসিকতা গড়ে তুলুন আর নেতিবাচক চিন্তা এড়িয়ে চলুন। সাফল্যের জন্য কঠোর পরিশ্রম জরুরি।"

### **The 90-10 Rule**
**90% Bengali, 10% English (only unavoidable terms)**

## **BENGALI LINGUISTIC NUANCES**

### **1. Sadhu Bhasha vs Cholito Bhasha**

**CRITICAL:** Use Cholito Bhasha (modern spoken Bengali), NOT Sadhu Bhasha (classical/literary Bengali).

**❌ Sadhu Bhasha (AVOID):**
- করিতে হইবে → ✓ করতে হবে
- যাইতেছি → ✓ যাচ্ছি
- করিয়াছি → ✓ করেছি

**✓ Cholito Bhasha (USE):**
- করতে হবে (have to do)
- যাচ্ছি (going)
- করেছি (have done)

### **2. য-ব Distinction**

Bengali has য and ব which sound similar but are different letters. Use correctly.

**Correct usage:**
- বাংলা (Bengali language) - with ব
- যাওয়া (going) - with য
- বলা (saying) - with ব
- যেতে (to go) - with য

### **3. Avoid Excessive Sanskrit**

While Bengali has Sanskrit influence, don't overuse Sanskritized words when simpler Bengali exists.

**❌ Too Sanskritized:**
- প্রারম্ভ → ✓ শুরু
- সমাপ্তি → ✓ শেষ / সমাপ্তি (both acceptable)
- প্রয়োজন → ✓ দরকার / প্রয়োজন (both acceptable)

**✓ Natural Bengali:**
- শুরু (beginning)
- শেষ (end)
- দরকার (need)

### **4. Plural Forms**

Bengali plurals are often formed by adding -গুলি or -গুলো (informal).

- লক্ষ্য → লক্ষ্যগুলি / লক্ষ্যগুলো (goals)
- চিন্তা → চিন্তাগুলি / চিন্তাগুলো (thoughts)
- পথ → পথগুলি / পথগুলো (ways)

### **5. Question Formation**

Bengali questions often use কি, কেন, কীভাবে.

- আপনি জানেন কি? (Do you know?)
- কী সমস্যা? (What is the problem?)
- কীভাবে করবেন? (How will you do?)

### **6. Verb Conjugation**

**Formal (আপনি):**
- আপনি করুন (you do - imperative)
- আপনি করতে পারেন (you can do)
- আপনার করা উচিত (you should do)

**Informal (তুমি):**
- তুমি করো (you do)
- তুমি করতে পারো (you can do)
- তোমার করা উচিত (you should do)

## **TONE & VOICE: WARM AND ENCOURAGING**

### **Self-Help Tone Requirements:**

**✓ DO:**
- Use আপনি for respectful warmth (recommended)
- Sound encouraging: "আপনি এটা অবশ্যই পারবেন!"
- Use rhetorical questions: "ভেবে দেখুন তো, আপনি আসলে কী চান?"
- Share wisdom like a caring elder
- Be direct but kind
- Use Bengali vocabulary predominantly

**❌ DON'T:**
- Sound preachy
- Use Sadhu Bhasha (classical Bengali)
- Use excessive Sanskrit
- Pepper text with English words

---

## **SIMPLICITY RULES**

### **Rule 1: Short Sentences**
One idea per sentence. Bengali readers prefer digestible wisdom.

### **Rule 2: Active Voice**
Prefer active constructions.

**English:** "Fear is experienced by everyone."  
**❌ Passive:** "ভয় সবার দ্বারা অনুভব করা হয়।"  
**✓ Active:** "সবার ভয় লাগে।"

### **Rule 3: Concrete Examples**
Use concrete examples over abstract concepts.

### **Rule 4: Engaging Questions**
Use rhetorical questions to engage.

---

## **QUALITY CHECK**

**After translating each paragraph:**
1. Count English words (excluding brand names)
2. If more than 1-2 per paragraph → too much English
3. Check for Sadhu Bhasha - replace with Cholito Bhasha
4. Ask: Would a typical Bengali reader understand easily?

---

## **FORBIDDEN PATTERNS**

**❌ NEVER:**
1. Use Sadhu Bhasha when Cholito Bhasha is appropriate
2. Translate word-by-word maintaining English structure
3. Keep English words when Bengali alternatives exist
4. Create Banglish by mixing too much English
5. Use excessive Sanskrit
6. Sound like a textbook

## **FINAL PRINCIPLES**

### **Priority Hierarchy:**
1. **Use Bengali First** > Always default to Bengali
2. **Use Cholito Bhasha** > NOT Sadhu Bhasha
3. **Consistency Throughout** > Everything else
4. **Natural Flow** > Literal accuracy
5. **Bengali Grammar Logic** > English structure
6. **Warmth & Connection** > Formal correctness
7. **Minimize English to <5%** > Non-negotiable

### **The Ultimate Test:**
**"Would my Bengali-speaking friend understand this easily and feel encouraged?"**

If yes → good translation  
If no → revise
