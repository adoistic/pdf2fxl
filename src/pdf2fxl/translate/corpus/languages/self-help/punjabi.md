# **Translation Instructions for Self-Help Book: English to Natural Punjabi**

## **YOUR MISSION**
Translate this English self-help book into **natural, flowing Punjabi** that feels like a caring friend talking to you. This is NOT an academic exercise; your goal is to create a reading experience that is warm, encouraging, and effortlessly understood by everyday Punjabi readers.

**Target audience:** Regular Punjabi-speaking people in Punjab (India/Pakistan) who read for personal growth—not scholars. They want simple wisdom in natural Punjabi.

---

## **CRITICAL: CONSISTENCY IS NON-NEGOTIABLE**

You are creating ONE unified reading experience. Every choice must remain consistent throughout the ENTIRE book.

**The reader should feel:**
"ਇਹ ਇੱਕ ਵਿਅਕਤੀ ਦੀ ਆਵਾਜ਼ ਹੈ ਜੋ ਮੈਨੂੰ ਇਸ ਸਫ਼ਰ ਵਿੱਚ ਮਾਰਗਦਰਸ਼ਨ ਕਰ ਰਹੀ ਹੈ।"

### **Maintain Your Translation Decisions:**

1. **Terminology Consistency:** Track and reuse translations
2. **Pronoun Consistency:** Choose ਤੁਸੀਂ (formal) OR ਤੂੰ (informal)—never switch
3. **Tone Consistency:** Maintain warmth throughout

---

## **CRITICAL WARNING: AVOID PUNGLISH TRAP**

**❌ PUNGLISH (WRONG):**
"ਤੁਹਾਨੂੰ goals set ਕਰਨੇ ਪੈਣਗੇ ਅਤੇ daily routine ਵਿੱਚ discipline maintain ਕਰਨਾ ਪਵੇਗਾ।"

**✓ NATURAL PUNJABI (CORRECT):**
"ਤੁਹਾਨੂੰ ਆਪਣੇ ਟੀਚੇ ਨਿਰਧਾਰਤ ਕਰਨੇ ਪੈਣਗੇ ਅਤੇ ਰੋਜ਼ਾਨਾ ਜੀਵਨ ਵਿੱਚ ਅਨੁਸ਼ਾਸਨ ਬਣਾਈ ਰੱਖਣਾ ਪਵੇਗਾ।"

### **The 90-10 Rule**
**90% Punjabi, 10% English (only unavoidable terms)**

## **PUNJABI LINGUISTIC NUANCES**

### **1. Gurmukhi Script**
Use proper Gurmukhi script with correct matras and tippis.

### **2. Tones**
Punjabi is a tonal language. While written form doesn't show tones, word choice affects natural reading.

### **3. Avoid Hindi Influence**
Punjabi and Hindi are different. Use authentic Punjabi vocabulary.

**✓ Natural Punjabi:**
- ਸ਼ੁਰੂਆਤ (beginning)
- ਸਫਲਤਾ (success)
- ਸਮਾਂ (time)
- ਵਿਚਾਰ (thought)
- ਜ਼ਰੂਰੀ (necessary)

---

## **TONE & VOICE: WARM AND ENCOURAGING**

**✓ DO:**
- Use ਤੁਸੀਂ for respectful warmth
- Sound encouraging: "ਤੁਸੀਂ ਇਹ ਜ਼ਰੂਰ ਕਰ ਸਕਦੇ ਹੋ!"
- Use rhetorical questions
- Be direct but kind

**❌ DON'T:**
- Sound preachy
- Use Hindi words when Punjabi alternatives exist
- Pepper text with English words

---

## **FINAL PRINCIPLES**

1. **Use Punjabi First** > Always default to Punjabi (NOT Hindi)
2. **Consistency Throughout** > Everything else
3. **Natural Flow** > Literal accuracy
4. **Minimize English to <5%** > Non-negotiable

**Create a Punjabi self-help book that feels originally written in Punjabi—warm, consistent, and free of unnecessary English.** 📖✨
