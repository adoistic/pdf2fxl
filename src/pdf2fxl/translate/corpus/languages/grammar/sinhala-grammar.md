# Critical Grammar Concepts for Sinhala (සිංහල) Translation

## 1. Diglossia & Verbs - **CORE SYNTAX**

Spoken Sinhala has unified verb endings.
- **Mama yanawa** (I go).
- **Api yanawa** (We go).
- **Instruction:** Use **Colloquial Standard** for modern prose.

---

## 2. Inanimate vs Animate Split

Grammar changes based on life.
- **Inanimate Locative:** **-e** (Pothe - in book).
- **Animate Locative:** **-gava** (Lamaya-gava - with child).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Sinhala

When translating into Sinhala, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Animacy Check:** Is the noun/object alive? Choose the correct locative and plural suffixes (**-e vs -gava**).
2. **Indefinite Marker:** Handle "a/an" with the **-ak** (inanimate) or **-ek** (animate) suffix at the end of the noun.
3. **Register Alignment:** Avoid 'Yami/Yamu' literary endings. Stick to the **-nawa** (present) or **-la** (past) spoken forms.
4. **Identity Copula:** In sentences of identity (A is B), **Drop the verb** entirely. (e.g., "Mama guruvarayek" - I am a teacher).
5. **Emphasis:** Use **'-u'** or **'-ma'** particles attached to the noun for "also" or "specifically".

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Identity
- **English:** "He is my doctor."
- **✓ Natural:** "**එයා මගේ දොස්තර।**"

### Example 2: Possession
- **English:** "Ram's house."
- **✓ Natural:** "**රාමගේ ගෙදර।**"

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
