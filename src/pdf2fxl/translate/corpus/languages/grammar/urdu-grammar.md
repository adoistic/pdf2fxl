# Critical Grammar Concepts for Urdu (اردو) Translation

## 1. Izafat (اضافت) - **CORE SYNTAX**

Izafat connects nouns using a short 'i' sound (Zer). It is the soul of formal/literary Urdu.
- **English:** "Voice of the heart."
- **✓ Natural:** "**آوازِ دل**" (Awaaz-e-dil).

---

## 2. Adab (Respect Levels)

Urdu grammar is dictated by politeness.
- **Respectful:** "**تشریف لائیے**" (Tashreef laiye - Please come).
- **Verb agreement:** Verbs for respected subjects MUST be **Plural** (Hain).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Urdu

When translating into Urdu, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Izafat Opportunity:** Can two nouns be connected via **Izafat**? (e.g., Awaaz-e-Dil vs Dil ki Awaaz). Choose Izafat for a more professional tone.
2. **Adab Check:** Identify the subject's status. If respected, use **Plural Verb** agreements and honorific nouns (e.g., Tashreef).
3. **Vocabulary Choice:** Use Persianate/Arabic plurals (**Broken Plurals**) like 'Auqaat' or 'Afkaar' for intellectual contexts.
4. **Gender Trace:** Verify the gender of inanimate nouns (e.g., 'Kitaab' is Fem). All adjectives and verbs must agree.
5. **Ergativity Check:** In past transitive sentences, ensure the subject has **'Ne'** and the verb matches the object.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Formal Address
- **English:** "Please sit."
- **✓ Natural:** "**تشریف رکھیئے**" (Tashreef rakhiye).

### Example 2: Possession
- **English:** "My father's friend."
- **✓ Natural:** "**میرے والد کے دوست**" (Mere walid ke dost).

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
