# Critical Grammar Concepts for Marathi (मराठी) Translation

## 1. Ergativity (The 'Ne' Rule) - **CORE SYNTAX**

In the past tense, transitive verbs agree with the **Object**, and the Subject takes **-ne**.
- **Subject marked:** **तीने** (Ti-ne - She).
- **Agreement:** "**तीने आंबा खाल्ला**" (Mango is Masc, so verb is Masc).
- **Neuter Objects:** "**तीने पत्र लिहिले**" (Letter is Neuter, so verb is Neuter).

---

## 2. The Letter ळ (Retroflex L)

Marathi is distinct from Hindi because of **ळ**. Using 'L' where 'LL' (ळ) is needed is a sign of poor translation.
- **Common words:** वेळ (Time), काळा (Black), निळा (Blue), पाऊस (Rain - related words).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Marathi

When translating into Marathi, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Transitive Past Check:** Is the action in the past? Is there an object? If yes, apply **'-ne'** to the subject and ignore it for verb agreement.
2. **Object Gender Sync:** Identify the Object's gender (M/F/N). Ensure the past tense verb suffix matches the Object, not the Subject.
3. **Oblique Stem Shift:** Before adding any postposition (at/t/madhye), transform the noun stem (Ghar -> **Ghara**-t).
4. **Retroflex L Audit:** Check all L sounds. Should it be the Marathi **ळ** (e.g., in 'Kaḷa' or 'Piwḷa')?
5. **Abstract Neuter:** For general "It/This" subjects, use the **Neuter** forms (Te/Changla).

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Ergative Agreement
- **English:** "Ram read the letter."
- **✓ Natural:** "**रामाने पत्र वाचले।**" ('Patra' is Neuter, 'Wachle' is Neuter).

### Example 2: Relationship
- **English:** "Rama's brother."
- **✓ Natural:** "**रामाचा भाऊ**" (Oblique 'Rama' + cha).

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
