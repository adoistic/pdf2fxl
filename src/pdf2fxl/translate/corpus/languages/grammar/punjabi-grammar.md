# Critical Grammar Concepts for Punjabi (ਪੰਜਾਬੀ) Translation

## 1. Pronoun Fusion - **CORE SYNTAX**

Punjabi merges pronouns and markers into single phonetic units.
- **English:** "To me."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ਮੈਨੂੰ**" (Mainu). (Not ਮੈਂ ਨੂੰ).
- **English:** "To you."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ਤੁਹਾਨੂੰ**" (Tuhanu). (Not ਤੁਸੀ ਨੂੰ).

---

## 2. Authentic Vocabulary vs Hindi Influence

Punjabi is frequently contaminated with Hindi cognates by LLMs.
- **Avoid:** Aur (और) -> **Use:** **ਅਤੇ / ਤੇ** (Atte / Te).
- **Avoid:** Ladki (लड़की) -> **Use:** **ਕੁੜੀ** (Kudi).
- **Avoid:** Samay (समय) -> **Use:** **ਸਮਾਂ** (Samaa).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Punjabi

When translating into Punjabi, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Fusion Audit:** Check all "Subject + To/For" combinations (Main/Tusi/Oh + nu). Ensure they are **Fused** (Mainu/Tuhanu/Ohnu).
2. **Vocabulary Filter:** Did you use 'Aur' or 'Samay'? If yes, replace with **'Te/Atte'** and **'Samaa'**.
3. **Oblique Check:** For masculine nouns ending in 'a' (e.g., Munda), shift to 'e' before a postposition (**Munde nu**).
4. **Continuous Aspect:** Use colloquial **'Pya'** structures for ongoing actions (Oh karda pya - "He is doing") instead of formal 'Raha'.
5. **Nasality Check:** Ensure feminine plural nouns keep their terminal nasal dot (Bindi) for proper verb agreement.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Continuous Action
- **English:** "They are coming."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ਉਹ ਆਉਂਦੇ ਪਏ ਨੇ।**"

### Example 2: Relationship
- **English:** "Friend's book."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ਦੋਸਤ ਦੀ ਕਿਤਾਬ।**"

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
