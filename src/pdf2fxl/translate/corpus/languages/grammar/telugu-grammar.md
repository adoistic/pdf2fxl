# Critical Grammar Concepts for Telugu (తెలుగు) Translation

## 1. Vibhakti & Case Hierarchy - **CORE SYNTAX**

Telugu uses suffixes (Vibhaktis) for all relations.
- **Shashti (Possession):** Usually indicated by noun placement or stem shift. **Drop 'yokka'** (యొక్క) in natural prose.
  - "Ram's house" -> "**రాము ఇల్లు**" (Not రాము యొక్క ఇల్లు).

---

## 2. Sandhi (Vowel Merging)

Mandatory vowel fusion in spoken/natural Telugu.
- **Meeku** (to you) + **Em** (what) -> **Meekem**.
- **Vaadu** + **Unnadu** -> **Vaadunnadu**.

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Telugu

When translating into Telugu, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Possession Logic:** Did you use 'yokka'? If so, **remove it** and handle possession via stem shift or proximity.
2. **Subject Transformation:** For feelings/needs, convert the subject to **Dative** (Naaku/Meeku).
3. **Sandhi Audit:** Are words phonetically merged? Ensure vowels are fused at the end of noun-verb or pronoun-verb boundaries.
4. **Respect Hierarchy:** Are you using **'Aayana'** and **'Garu'** for respected subjects? Keep the plural verb agreement consistent.
5. **Register Shift:** Avoid Grandhika (bookish) endings. Stick to **Vyavaharika** (Modern Modern) forms.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Respectful Address
- **English:** "He is my father."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ఆయన మా నాన్నగారు।**"

### Example 2: Wanting
- **English:** "I want this."
- **✓ Natural:** "**నాకు ఇది కావాలి।**"

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
