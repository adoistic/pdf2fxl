# Critical Grammar Concepts for Assamese (অসমীয়া) Translation

## 1. Classifier Necessity - **CORE SYNTAX**

Assamese verbs and nouns are incomplete without classifiers.
- **Specific Object:** **কিতাপখন** (Kitap-khon - The book).
- **Reasoning:** 'Khon' is for flat objects.

---

## 2. Prefixed Negation

Negative markers are prefixed and change based on vowel harmony.
- **English:** "I don't go."
- **✓ Natural:** "**মই নাযাম**" (Nazam).
- **English:** "I don't do."
- **✓ Natural:** "**মই নকৰোঁ**" (Nokoron).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Assamese

When translating into Assamese, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Classifier Selection:** Identify the noun category (Flat/Human/Long). Apply the matching **Classifier** suffix (khon/zon/to).
2. **Negation Fusion:** Ensure the negative marker 'Na-' is a **Prefix** to the verb. Check for **Vowel Harmony** (Na- vs No- shifts).
3. **Honorific Level:** Choose between Apuni (Formal) and Tumi (Informal). Maintain complete consistency across pronouns and verb endings.
4. **Relative Action:** Avoid "who/which". Place the **Participial Verb** before the noun (e.g., "Oha manuh-zon" for "The man who came").
5. **Article Removal:** Strip 'Eta' (a) unless the specific number one is intended.
