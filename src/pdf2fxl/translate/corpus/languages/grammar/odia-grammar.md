# Critical Grammar Concepts for Odia (ଓଡ଼ିଆ) Translation

## 1. Classifiers (Ta/Ti/Tie) - **CORE SYNTAX**

Odia uses classifiers for definiteness and count.
- **English:** "A bird" -> **ପକ୍ଷୀଟିଏ** (Pakshi-tie).
- **English:** "The book" -> **ବହିଟି** (Bohi-ti).

---

## 2. Honorifics & Respect

Odia has strict honorific verb agreements.
- **Formal:** **କରୁଛନ୍ତି** (Karuchanti - Respectful 'Doing').
- **Standard:** **କରୁଛ** (Karucha).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Odia

When translating into Odia, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Classifier Mapping:** Does the noun require a classifier? Use **'-ti'** for specific singular, **'-ta'** for generic, and **'-tie'** for indefinite ("a").
2. **Postposition Fusion:** Ensure all postpositions (lo, re, ru, ku) are **Attached** to the noun stem without spaces.
3. **Honorific Consistency:** Check the respect level. If 'Apana' is the subject, ensure the verb ending is **'-chanti'** or equivalent.
4. **Subject Case:** For internal states (hunger, thirst), use the **Dative Subject** logic (Mote - "To me").
5. **Article Stripping:** Remove 'Ek' (one) unless numerical emphasis is the primary goal.
