# Critical Grammar Concepts for Tamil (தமிழ்) Translation

## 1. Agglutination & Sandhi - **CORE SYNTAX**

Tamil is highly agglutinative. Suffixes stack onto the noun to denote case, number, and relation.

### The Rule of Joining
- **English:** "In the house" -> 3 words.
- **Tamil:** "**வீட்டில்**" (Veettil) -> 1 word. 
- **Sandhi:** Sounds change when words join (e.g., Petti + il -> **பெட்டிக்குள்** with 'kk' buffer).

---

## 2. Diglossia: Written vs Spoken

Tamil has a major gap between formal/bookish Tamil and natural spoken Tamil.
- **Formal:** இல்லை (Illai).
- **Spoken:** **இல்ல** (Illa).
- **Instruction:** Use **Formal Spoken** for modern self-help. Polite but not archaic.

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Tamil

When translating into Tamil, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Suffix Stacking:** Are you using separate words for prepositions? If so, fuse them into the noun using the correct **Agglutination** chain.
2. **Sandhi Check:** When merging words, did you add the required transition consonants (e.g., k, ch, th, p doubling)?
3. **Register Alignment:** Is the verb ending too literary (e.g., -kiraar) or too slangy? Aim for the **Standard Formal** middle ground.
4. **Relative Clause Check:** Avoid "who/which". Turn the action into an adjective **before** the noun (e.g., "Came man" instead of "The man who came").
5. **Null Copula:** Did you add an unnecessary "is" (irukkiraar)? If it's a simple identity sentence (He is a doctor), remove it.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Article Omission
- **English:** "I saw a tree."
- **✓ Natural:** "**நான் மரம் பார்த்தேன்.**"

### Example 2: Complex Sandhi
- **English:** "Inside the box."
- **✓ Natural:** "**பெட்டிக்குள்**" (Not பெட்டி இல்).

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
