# Critical Grammar Concepts for Malayalam (മലയാളം) Translation

## 1. Dative Subject Construction - **CORE SYNTAX**

Feelings and mental states "happen to" you in Malayalam. You are the object in the Dative case.
- **English:** "I want coffee."
- **✓ Natural:** "**എനിക്ക് കാപ്പി വേണം**" (Enikku kaappi venam - "To me coffee is needed").

---

## 2. Agglutination & Sandhi

Malayalam merges words meticulously.
- **Rule:** Vowels never meet. Add a bridge letter.
  - **Amma** + **ude** -> **Ammayude** (Mother's).
  - **Poo** + **ile** -> **Poovile** (In the flower).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Malayalam

When translating into Malayalam, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **State Analysis:** Is the sentence about a feeling, need, or knowledge? If so, uses **'Enikku'** (Dative Subject) logic.
2. **Gliding Connectives:** When two vowels meet (e.g., Noun + Case), did you insert the correct bridge (**y or v**)?
3. **Consonant Doubling:** Check locatives. Did you double the consonant correctly? (Veedu -> **Veettil**).
4. **Passive Avoidance:** Did you use the active voice? Avoid "eppettu" (was done) structures; they are highly unnatural.
5. **Article Removal:** Strip 'Oru' (a) unless the specific number 'one' is intended.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Possession
- **English:** "My father's house."
- **✓ Natural:** "**എന്റെ അച്ഛന്റെ വീട്**" (Ente achante veedu).

### Example 2: Identity
- **English:** "This is a book."
- **✓ Natural:** "**ഇതൊരു പുസ്തകമാണ്**" (Ithoru pusthakamaanu).

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
