# Critical Grammar Concepts for Nepali (नेपाली) Translation

## 1. Ergative Marker (-le) - **CORE SYNTAX**

Nepali uses **'-le'** for agents of transitive verbs.
- **Rule:** If the verb is transitive and in the past, add **'-le'** to the subject.
- **Example:** "**मैले काम गरें**" (Ma-le ... - "I did the work").

---

## 2. 'Ho' vs 'Cha' Distinction

Use 'Ho' for identity and 'Cha' for state or location.
- **Identity:** "**उ डाक्टर हो**" (He IS a doctor).
- **Location:** "**उ विद्यालयमा छ**" (He IS at school).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Nepali

When translating into Nepali, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Ergativity Check:** Is the action in the past? Is the verb transitive? If yes, apply the **'-le'** marker to the subject.
2. **Copula Selection:** Are you defining something (Ho) or stating its location/status (Cha)? Choose the correct **'To Be'** verb.
3. **Honorific Tier:** Identify the audience tier (**Tapai** for formal/standard). Align all verb endings with this level.
4. **Classifier usage:** For "a/an", use **'Euta'** (objects) or **'Eujana'** (people) instead of a literal 'Ek'.
5. **Conjunctive Suffix:** Join sequential actions using **'-era'** (e.g., kha-era - "after eating") instead of 'and'.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Defining a person
- **English:** "Ram is a student."
- **✓ Natural:** "**राम विद्यार्थी हो।**"

### Example 2: Sequential action
- **English:** "I came and saw."
- **✓ Natural:** "**म आएर देखें।**"

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
