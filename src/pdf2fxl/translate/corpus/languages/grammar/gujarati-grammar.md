# Critical Grammar Concepts for Gujarati Translation

## ⚠️ CRITICAL: Translation is Context-Dependent, NOT Literal

### **Most Important Rule: Think in Gujarati, Don't Translate Word-by-Word**

**South Asian languages are HIGHLY contextual.** Every word choice depends on the implied meaning and cultural patterns.

---

## 1. Krudant (કૃદંત) - **CORE SYNTAX**

Krudants are non-finite verb forms that act as nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. 

### Key Types
- **Nāmak Krudant (-vu):** The action as a noun. (Reading -> વાંચન / વાંચવું).
- **Viśeṣaṇ Krudant (-elo/eli/elu):** The action as an adjective. Must agree with the noun's gender.
  - **તૂટેલો પ્યાલો** (Broken glass - M)
  - **તૂટેલી બારી** (Broken window - F)
- **Sequential Krudant (-ine):** Action done before another. (After eating -> ખાઈને).

---

## 2. Gender Agreement (M, F, N)

Gujarati has three genders. The **Neuter (N)** gender is used for many inanimate objects and abstract concepts.
- **Saaro** (Good - M)
- **Saari** (Good - F)
- **Saaru** (Good - N) -> **સાચું કામ** (Correct work).

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Gujarati

When translating into Gujarati, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Gender Check:** Identify the noun's gender (M/F/N). Ensure all adjectives and Krudants agree with it.
2. **Krudant Selection:** Is the action a description? Use **Viśeṣaṇ Krudant**. Is it a sequential action? Use **-ine** suffix.
3. **Oblique shift:** Check for postpositions (ne, thi, ma, par). If a noun ends in 'o' or 'u', shift it to 'a' (Chokro -> Chokra ne).
4. **Article Stripping:** Remove English articles (a/an/the) unless specific count is the focus.
5. **Register Check:** Is the context formal? Use 'Tame' and respectful verb endings.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

### Example 1: Action Chain
- **English:** "I went home after eating."
- **✓ Natural:** "**હું ખાઈને ઘરે ગયો।**"

### Example 2: Neuter Identity
- **English:** "It is true."
- **✓ Natural:** "**તે સાચું છે।**"

**Your goal: NATURAL, CONTEXTUAL translation - not perfect rule-following.**
