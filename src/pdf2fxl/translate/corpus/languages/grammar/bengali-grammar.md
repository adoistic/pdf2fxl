# Critical Grammar Concepts for Bengali (বাংলা) Translation

## 1. Classifiers (Ta/Ti/Jon) - **CORE SYNTAX**

Bengali uses classifiers to indicate definiteness and category. 

### Usage Logic
- **-ta / -ti:** Generic objects, small animals, children. (-ti is softer).
- **-jon:** Respectful for humans.
- **-khana:** Flat/large objects like books or sheets.

**Rule:** "The book" -> **বইটা** (Boita). Without '-ta', it's just general "book".

---

## 2. Zero Copula (Absence of "Is")

Bengali typically omits the verb "to be" in present tense identity sentences.
- **English:** "She is a student."
- **✓ Natural:** "**সে একজন ছাত্রী**" (Se ekjon chatri - literally "She one student").

---

## 3. Linguistic Decision Protocol: Bengali

When translating into Bengali, follow this specific thought sequence:

1. **Definiteness Check:** Does the English sentence imply "THE object"? If yes, append the correct **Classifier** (-ta, -ti, -khana).
2. **Honorific Selection:** Identify the respect level (Apni/Tumi/Tui). Keep verb endings and pronouns perfectly **consistent** throughout.
3. **Copula Check:** Are you using 'hoy' or 'achhe' for a simple definition? If yes, **remove it**.
4. **State Construction:** For health/feelings (cold, hunger), use the **Genitive Subject** (Amar thanda lagche - "To me cold is sticking").
5. **Emphasis:** Use '-i' (only) or '-o' (also) as suffixes directly attached to the noun.

## Few-Shot Examples (Context-Aware)

- **English:** "Bring the book."
