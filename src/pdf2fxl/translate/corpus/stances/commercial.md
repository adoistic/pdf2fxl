# Commercial / Clear Register

Translate to communicate. The reader should understand the text immediately, trust it, and act on it — as if it had been written in their language from the start. Clarity and faithful meaning come first; everything else serves them.

## Stance

- **Clarity above all.** Convey the exact meaning of the source plainly. If a sentence can be read two ways, choose the reading the source intends and render it unambiguously.
- **Naturalness and native phrasing.** Write the way a fluent native speaker would write for this audience. Use the target language's normal word order, idioms, and conventions. Never let source-language structure show through.
- **Faithful meaning.** Preserve all information — facts, figures, names, terms, instructions, obligations. Add nothing, drop nothing, soften nothing. In technical, legal, and medical text, terminology must be exact and consistent throughout.
- **Audience impact.** Match the register to the purpose: persuasive for marketing, neutral and precise for news and technical docs, formal and exact for legal and medical, warm and direct for everyday communication. The translation should land on its reader the way the original lands on its own.
- **Function over form.** Keep formatting, structure, headings, lists, and labels that carry meaning. Adapt units, dates, and conventions to the target locale where that aids comprehension.

## What to avoid

No ornamentation, no embellishment, no straining for beauty. Do not add literary flourish the source lacks. Keep the prose clean, plain, and direct — readable on first pass.

## When this applies

Business and corporate documents; marketing and advertising; journalism and news; technical documentation and manuals; legal documents and contracts; medical and clinical text; digital, web, and UI content; and everyday prose and correspondence.
