# Cultural Translation (Meaning-Based Adaptation)

For creative, personal, philosophical, religious, and media/communications
content, translate for **meaning and cultural resonance**, not word-for-word.
Each of these categories carries a cultural bridge-building framework plus
category-specific guidance.

## The Cultural Bridge-Building Framework

The core instruction that prefixes every cultural category: treat the **target
language as a DISTINCT CULTURAL SYSTEM** with its own traditions, patterns, and
expressions — NOT a mere code for the source language. Each category specializes
the framework with its own AVOID/EMBRACE pairing and a WRONG/RIGHT example. Keep
these examples in mind verbatim — they carry the nuance.

---

## Creative Content

Applies to: Creative Writing, Screenplay, Short Story, Novel, Fiction, Creative
Fiction, Drama, Theater Script, Monologue, Dialogue.

Base instruction: Translate for MEANING and CULTURAL RESONANCE, not word-for-word.
Focus on conveying the emotional impact, artistic intent, and creative expression
rather than literal linguistic conversion.

Per-type guidance:
- **Creative Writing** — Preserve the author's unique voice, style, and creative
  choices. Maintain narrative flow, character development, and atmospheric
  elements. Adapt cultural references to resonate with the target-language readers
  while preserving the original's artistic integrity.
- **Screenplay** — Preserve dialogue naturalness, character voice distinctions,
  and dramatic timing. Adapt humor, wordplay, and cultural references for the
  target audience. Maintain subtext, emotional beats, and visual storytelling.
- **Short Story** — Capture the author's narrative style, tone, and literary
  devices. Preserve mood, atmosphere, and character psychology. Adapt cultural
  nuances while maintaining the story's emotional core and thematic depth.
- **Novel** — Maintain narrative consistency, character development arcs, and
  thematic coherence across chapters. Preserve the author's distinctive voice
  while making cultural references accessible. Emotional resonance over literal
  accuracy.
- **Default** — Prioritize artistic expression, emotional impact, and creative
  intent over literal word-for-word conversion.

Cultural bridge-building (Creative):

> Treat the target language as a DISTINCT CULTURAL SYSTEM with its own literary
> traditions, narrative patterns, and artistic expressions — NOT as a mere code
> for the source language. Target readers have different cultural contexts,
> storytelling expectations, and emotional triggers.
>
> AVOID: Word-for-word substitution, maintaining source language sentence
> structures, preserving idioms that don't work in the target language, keeping
> cultural references that will confuse target readers.
>
> EMBRACE: Target-language narrative flow, culturally appropriate metaphors for
> target audiences, target-language storytelling conventions, emotional
> expressions that resonate naturally with target speakers.
>
> Example of WRONG approach: "His heart was like a butterfly in his chest" →
> Direct translation maintaining the exact metaphor even if butterflies have
> different cultural significance.
>
> Example of RIGHT approach: Transform the metaphor to match target-language
> cultural associations while preserving the emotional intent — perhaps "His
> heart fluttered like cherry blossoms in spring wind" for Japanese, or "His
> heart raced like a wild stallion" for Spanish, depending on cultural resonance.

## Personal Content

Applies to: Personal Letter, Diary/Journal, Memoir, Love Letter, Personal Essay,
Autobiography, Personal Narrative.

Base instruction: Translate for EMOTIONAL AUTHENTICITY and PERSONAL MEANING, not
literal word conversion. Focus on conveying the genuine human experience,
emotional nuances, and personal significance.

Per-type guidance:
- **Personal Letter** — Preserve the intimate tone, personal voice, and emotional
  relationship between writer and recipient. Maintain natural speech patterns and
  personal expressions that reflect the writer's personality. Adapt cultural
  context while preserving emotional authenticity.
- **Diary/Journal** — Capture the internal voice, personal reflections, and
  emotional honesty. Preserve the writer's thought patterns, personal style, and
  emotional journey. Maintain the intimate, often stream-of-consciousness nature.
- **Memoir** — Preserve personal narrative voice, emotional memory, and life
  experience authenticity. Balance personal reflection and broader human
  experience. Adapt cultural context while preserving universal human elements.
- **Love Letter** — Capture romantic sentiment, emotional vulnerability, and
  intimate expression. Preserve the tender voice, personal endearments, and
  emotional intensity. Adapt cultural expressions of love while maintaining
  genuine feeling.
- **Default** — Prioritize emotional truth, personal voice, and human connection
  over word-for-word literal translation.

Cultural bridge-building (Personal):

> Treat the target language as a DISTINCT CULTURAL SYSTEM with its own ways of
> expressing emotions, personal relationships, and intimate thoughts — NOT as a
> mere code for the source language. Target speakers have different cultural norms
> for personal expression, emotional intimacy, and relationship dynamics.
>
> AVOID: Direct translation of personal expressions, maintaining source language
> formality levels that seem unnatural, preserving emotional metaphors that don't
> resonate, keeping cultural relationship concepts that confuse target readers.
>
> EMBRACE: Target-language natural emotional expressions, culturally appropriate
> intimacy levels, target-language relationship dynamics, personal communication
> styles that feel authentic to target speakers.
>
> Example of WRONG approach: "I miss you like the desert misses rain" → Direct
> translation even if desert metaphors have no cultural relevance.
>
> Example of RIGHT approach: Transform to match target-language cultural emotional
> expressions — "I miss you like spring misses its flowers" for cultures that
> value spring imagery, or "I miss you like the ocean misses the moon" for coastal
> cultures, while preserving the deep longing sentiment.

## Philosophical Content

Applies to: Philosophy, Ethics, Metaphysics, Political Philosophy, Philosophical
Essay, Philosophical Treatise, Logic, Epistemology.

Base instruction: Translate for CONCEPTUAL CLARITY and PHILOSOPHICAL MEANING, not
surface-level word substitution. Focus on conveying complex ideas, logical
arguments, and philosophical insights.

Per-type guidance:
- **Philosophy** — Preserve logical argumentation, conceptual precision, and
  philosophical reasoning. Maintain the coherence of philosophical systems and the
  development of complex ideas. Adapt terminology to established target-language
  philosophical traditions while preserving meaning.
- **Ethics** — Preserve moral reasoning, ethical frameworks, and value-based
  arguments. Maintain the logical structure of ethical discourse and the nuanced
  exploration of moral concepts. Adapt cultural ethical contexts while preserving
  universal moral insights.
- **Metaphysics** — Capture abstract concepts, ontological discussions, and
  fundamental questions about reality. Preserve philosophical rigor and conceptual
  depth. Use established target-language metaphysical vocabulary.
- **Political Philosophy** — Preserve political concepts, governmental theory, and
  social-contract ideas. Maintain the logical structure of political arguments
  while adapting cultural political contexts.
- **Default** — Prioritize conceptual understanding, logical coherence, and
  philosophical insight over literal linguistic conversion.

Cultural bridge-building (Philosophical):

> Treat the target language as a DISTINCT PHILOSOPHICAL TRADITION with its own
> conceptual frameworks, logical structures, and intellectual history — NOT as a
> mere code for the source language. Target-language philosophical discourse has
> different argumentative styles, conceptual hierarchies, and methodologies.
>
> AVOID: Direct translation of philosophical terms without context, maintaining
> source language logical structures that seem foreign, preserving conceptual
> metaphors that don't exist in the target tradition, keeping philosophical
> references that lack cultural grounding.
>
> EMBRACE: Target-language philosophical vocabulary and conceptual frameworks,
> established target intellectual traditions, logical argumentation styles natural
> to target thinkers, philosophical metaphors that resonate with target
> intellectual culture.
>
> Example of WRONG approach: "The soul is the form of the body" → Direct
> translation without considering how the target tradition conceptualizes
> soul-body relationships.
>
> Example of RIGHT approach: Engage with target-language philosophical traditions
> — if translating Aristotelian concepts to Chinese, connect with qi/li
> frameworks; if to Arabic, engage with nafs/ruh distinctions; if to German,
> utilize Geist/Körper conceptual relationships that German philosophy has
> developed.

## Religious Content

Applies to: Religious Text, Prayer, Sermon, Theological Essay, Spiritual
Meditation, Religious Commentary, Scripture, Devotional Text, Religious Poetry,
Hymn.

Base instruction: Translate for SPIRITUAL MEANING and RELIGIOUS SIGNIFICANCE, not
mechanical word replacement. Focus on conveying faith, devotion, spiritual
insights, and religious experience.

Per-type guidance:
- **Religious Text** — Preserve sacred meaning, spiritual teachings, and religious
  authority. Maintain reverent tone, doctrinal accuracy, and spiritual impact. Use
  established target-language religious vocabulary while preserving sacred meaning.
- **Prayer** — Capture devotional sentiment, spiritual connection, and sacred
  communication. Preserve the rhythm of prayer, reverent tone, and spiritual
  intention. Adapt cultural religious expressions while maintaining spiritual
  authenticity.
- **Sermon** — Preserve homiletic style, spiritual teaching, and inspirational
  message. Balance doctrinal instruction and pastoral care. Adapt cultural
  religious context while preserving universal spiritual truth.
- **Theological Essay** — Preserve theological reasoning, doctrinal precision, and
  spiritual scholarship. Maintain academic rigor while preserving the faith-based
  perspective and spiritual insights.
- **Spiritual Meditation** — Capture contemplative mood, spiritual reflection, and
  meditative peace. Preserve the gentle, introspective tone and spiritual guidance
  while maintaining the sacred atmosphere.
- **Default** — Prioritize spiritual truth, religious significance, and faith
  experience over literal word-by-word conversion.

Cultural bridge-building (Religious):

> Treat the target language as a DISTINCT SPIRITUAL TRADITION with its own
> religious vocabulary, devotional practices, and sacred expressions — NOT as a
> mere code for the source language. Target-language religious discourse has
> different prayer styles, theological frameworks, and spiritual metaphors rooted
> in its cultural context.
>
> AVOID: Direct translation of religious terms without spiritual context,
> maintaining source language liturgical structures that feel foreign, preserving
> spiritual metaphors that don't resonate, keeping religious references that lack
> cultural foundation in the target tradition.
>
> EMBRACE: Target-language established religious vocabulary and devotional
> expressions, target theological traditions and spiritual frameworks, prayer and
> worship styles natural to target faith communities, sacred metaphors that
> resonate with target spiritual culture.
>
> Example of WRONG approach: "God's grace flows like water" → Direct translation
> without considering how the target spiritual tradition conceptualizes divine
> grace.
>
> Example of RIGHT approach: Adapt to target-language spiritual metaphors — if
> translating to Arabic, engage with Islamic concepts of barakah/rahma; if to
> Sanskrit-influenced languages, connect with prasada/kripa concepts; if to
> indigenous languages, honor traditional spiritual metaphors while preserving the
> essence of divine blessing.

## Media & Communications Content

Applies to: News Article, Marketing Copy, Social Media Post, Press Release, Blog
Post, Public Speech, Advertisement, Editorial, Broadcast Script, Interview,
Article, Opinion Piece.

Base instruction: Translate for AUDIENCE IMPACT and COMMUNICATIVE EFFECTIVENESS,
not word-for-word literal conversion. Focus on conveying intended message,
audience engagement, and communication goals.

Per-type guidance:
- **News Article** — Preserve journalistic objectivity, factual accuracy, and news
  value. Maintain the inverted-pyramid structure and news writing style. Adapt
  cultural context and references for target audience understanding while
  preserving factual integrity.
- **Marketing Copy** — Capture persuasive intent, brand voice, and marketing
  objectives. Preserve call-to-action effectiveness and consumer appeal. Adapt
  cultural marketing approaches and consumer psychology for target markets while
  maintaining persuasive impact.
- **Social Media Post** — Preserve viral potential, engagement value, and social
  connection. Maintain conversational tone, emoji usage, and hashtag
  effectiveness. Adapt cultural social media norms and trending topics for target
  platforms.
- **Press Release** — Preserve professional tone, newsworthy information, and
  corporate messaging. Maintain the formal structure while adapting business
  culture and media-landscape expectations for target markets.
- **Blog Post** — Capture the blogger's voice, audience engagement, and
  informational value. Preserve the conversational yet informative tone while
  adapting cultural references and examples for target readers.
- **Public Speech** — Preserve oratorical impact, rhetorical devices, and audience
  connection. Maintain speech rhythm, persuasive elements, and emotional appeal
  while adapting cultural rhetorical traditions for target audiences.
- **Default** — Prioritize communicative effectiveness, audience engagement, and
  message impact over mechanical word substitution.

Cultural bridge-building (Media & Communications):

> Treat the target language as a DISTINCT COMMUNICATION CULTURE with its own media
> conventions, audience expectations, and persuasive techniques — NOT as a mere
> code for the source language. The target media landscape has different
> rhetorical styles, cultural references, and audience engagement patterns.
>
> AVOID: Direct translation of marketing phrases that don't resonate, maintaining
> source language communication styles that seem foreign, preserving cultural
> references that confuse target audiences, keeping persuasive techniques that
> don't work in the target culture.
>
> EMBRACE: Target-language native communication patterns and rhetorical devices,
> culturally appropriate persuasive techniques for target audiences, target media
> conventions and audience expectations, communication styles that feel natural
> and engaging to target speakers.
>
> Example of WRONG approach: "Think outside the box" → Direct translation of
> English idiom that may not exist or resonate in the target language.
>
> Example of RIGHT approach: Transform to target-language cultural expressions —
> "Break conventional thinking" for formal cultures, "Color outside the lines" for
> creative cultures, or "Find new paths" for cultures that value journey
> metaphors, while preserving the innovative thinking message.
